MEDICATIONS
-----------
1. Acetaminophen (paracetamol) 500–1000 mg every 6 hours as needed for pain. Maximum 3 g per 24 hours.
2. Ibuprofen 400 mg every 8 hours with food for the first 5 days.
3. Ondansetron 4 mg every 8 hours as needed for nausea.

You may be given a short course of oxycodone for breakthrough pain — use only if acetaminophen and ibuprofen are insufficient.

ACTIVITY
--------
- Walk and move around frequently from the day of surgery.
- Avoid heavy lifting (>15 lbs / 7 kg) for 1 week.
- Return to desk-based work when comfortable, usually 3–7 days.
- Resume driving once off opioid medication and able to perform an emergency stop.

WOUND CARE
----------
- Four small port-site incisions covered with steri-strips.
- Keep dry for 48 hours, then shower as normal.
- Mild bruising and tenderness around port sites is expected.

DIET
----
- Low-fat diet for the first 1–2 weeks to reduce GI symptoms.
- Reintroduce foods gradually; some patients experience temporary loose stools after cholecystectomy.
- Drink plenty of fluids.

NORMAL POST-OPERATIVE EXPERIENCES
---------------------------------
- Shoulder tip pain from CO2 used during surgery — resolves within 24–72 hours.
- Mild bloating.
- Tiredness for the first few days.

WHEN TO CALL THE SURGICAL TEAM
------------------------------
Contact us or attend A&E if you experience:
- Fever above 38°C.
- Worsening abdominal pain (especially right upper quadrant).
- Yellowing of skin or eyes (jaundice).
- Persistent nausea/vomiting beyond 24 hours.
- Wound redness, swelling, or pus.
- Dark urine or pale clay-coloured stools.
- Chest pain or shortness of breath.

FOLLOW-UP
---------
- Routine follow-up only if complications arise.
- GP review at 2 weeks if needed.
